About Us

CAVITRD (Centre for Advocacy and Visibility of Inclusive Transformation in Rural Disabilities) is a Tanzanian non-profit organization founded to empower children with disabilities in rural communities. We aim to reduce stigma, increase access to support, and improve the quality of life for children living with congenital and acquired disabilities.

Our Mission

To promote inclusive and equitable support systems for children with disabilities in underserved rural areas through education, advocacy, access to healthcare, and community-led solutions.

Our Objectives

  • Identify children with disabilities in rural communities and support their inclusion.
  • Educate families, caregivers, and leaders about early detection and care.
  • Increase access to rehabilitation, mobility aids, and medical referrals.
  • Promote inclusive policies through partnerships and advocacy.

Our Values

  • Inclusion: Every child deserves equal access and opportunity.
  • Dignity: We treat every individual with respect and compassion.
  • Empowerment: We support families to take the lead in care.
  • Accountability: We act with transparency and integrity.

Our History

CAVITRD began in Mwanza with a small group of passionate individuals — caregivers, health workers, and community leaders — who noticed a growing gap in support for rural children with disabilities. From early community workshops in Buswelu to formal partnerships with local clinics, our journey has always been rooted in compassion, inclusion, and visibility.
