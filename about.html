About CAVITRD

CAVITRD stands for Centre for Advocacy and Visibility of Inclusive Transformation in Rural Disabilities. CAVITRD is short for a registered Tanzanian NGO with Registration number 14NGO/R/4896, dealing with children and adults with congenital or acquired anomalies, vision impairment, and trauma-related disabilities.

We work to support children and adults with congenital or acquired anomalies, vision impairments, and trauma-related disabilities. Our efforts focus on rural communities, especially across the Lake Zone region of Tanzania.

Our Mission

To build inclusive and accessible systems of care for individuals with disabilities in underserved areas through education, healthcare, and advocacy.

Our Vision

A future where every person with a disability is visible, empowered, and included in all aspects of life.

Our Objectives

  • Enhance early identification and care for children with disabilities
  • Distribute assistive tools such as wheelchairs, walkers, and eyeglasses
  • Raise awareness in rural communities through outreach and education
  • Train caregivers and volunteers on disability support and rights
  • Advocate for inclusive education and health systems

Our History

CAVITRD began in Mwanza, where a group of caregivers, community leaders, and health workers saw how children with disabilities were being left behind. From a few small meetings and outreach sessions, we grew into a recognized organization. Our story continues with every village we reach, every family we support, and every child we help thrive.
